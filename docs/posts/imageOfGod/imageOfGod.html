The image and glory of God

 We are made in the image of God.

“And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth. So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them.” (Genesis 1:26-27).

Why does the Word say God made “man” in his own image, and continue to use “them” for the same subject in the next verse? Some translations use the alternate meaning of the word translated as “man” to express that it refers to mankind as a whole in this instance, including both man and woman. This would mean that man and woman were made in the image of God. At first, this may seem to contradict the next verse:

“So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them.” (Genesis 1:27).

This verse says that the man was made in the image of God without mentioning the woman. Why does it not say that God created both man and woman in his own image if the previous verse truly refers to both man and woman? Could it be that “man” referred only to adam, while “them” referred to his male descendants which were yet to come? Wouldn’t this mean woman has not been made in the image of God, which verse 27 can seem to agree with? We do get further clarification on this in chapter 2, when creation is covered in more detail. Let’s look more closely at how the woman was made.

“And the LORD God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam, and he slept: and he took one of his ribs, and closed up the flesh instead thereof; And the rib, which the LORD God had taken from man, made he a woman, and brought her unto the man. And Adam said, This is now bone of my bones, and flesh of my flesh: she shall be called Woman, because she was taken out of Man. Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh.” (Genesis 2:21-24).

We see here that woman was not made directly in the image of God as the man was, but as a part of the image of God, out of the man’s very flesh; this is why Genesis 1:27 doesn’t say that both man and woman were created in the image of God. Man was made in the image of God, and then woman was made from man as a part of his complete image, and mankind is the complete image of God. Woman was made, if you will, as a subset of the image of God, and this is why they’re to be joined together in marriage: to be one complete body. This means much more when we understand that we are reflections of God (since we are made in his image) and that Christ has his own bride in his body: those who are of faith, who he is one with in spirit. That Christ has his own bride in his people is also why Israel is considered to have committed spiritual adultery when worshiping idols[^je 3 8].
